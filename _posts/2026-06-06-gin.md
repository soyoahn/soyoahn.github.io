# How it works

<aside>
💡

The objective is to create **melds** in your hand and reduce “deadwood” (unmelded cards in hand). 

</aside>

<aside>

Valid **melds** you ****can create:

- **Straights**: **3+** of the same suit, in order. Aces are low.
    
    $$
    A^\clubs, 2^\clubs, 3^\clubs, 4^\clubs, 5^\clubs
    $$
    
- **Matches**: **3+** of a kind.
    
    $$
    2^\clubs, 2^\spades,2^{\color{red}{\text{♥}}}, 2^{\color{red}{\text{♦}}}
    $$
    
</aside>

<aside>

**Deadwood** is any unmelded card in your hand. You’ll want to discard deadwood strategically.

When a player ends the game with a knock or gin, the **deadwood of the losing player** is calculated into the winner’s points for that round.

Deadwood values are as follows:

- Face cards: 10pts
- Numbered cards have the same value as their number.

See END PHASE for how points are calculated.

</aside>

A player with no remaining deadwood declares “gin,” which is one way to end the game. The more common way to end is by “knocking”—more on this later. See END PHASE.

People typically play many rounds of gin in one sitting. A round goes pretty fast. You can keep score and continue playing until a player reaches 100 points.

# How to play

### ROUND START

1. **Dealing**: Previous round winner shuffles the deck and deals **10 cards face down** to each player.
2. **Setup**: 
    - The remaining deck is placed face down.
    - Take the top card and place it face up next to the deck. This is the **upcard**.
    
    <image, annotate deck and upcard>
    
3. **Housekeeping**: Players take a moment to organize and sort the cards in their hands.
4. **Determine first turn**: 
    
    Previous round loser has the first opportunity to start turn by drawing the **upcard** and complete their turn by proceeding to discard and pass turn.
    
    If they pass (it would be because they do not want the upcard), the same opportunity passes to the dealer.
    
    If the dealer passes on the upcard, the next player (previous round loser) starts turn.
    

### GAMEPLAY (TAKING TURNS)

The player must:

1. Draw 1 card. 
    
    Choose from EITHER the top of deck or the top of the upcard pile.
    
2. Discard 1 card.
    
    Place it face up on top of the upcard pile.
    
    **Tip**: Keep cards that meld or have the best chance of melding. You’ll want to discard your highest card that has the worst chance of melding.
    
3. Pass turn to next player.

<aside>
💡

Notice: You always begin and end a turn with exactly 10 cards in hand.

</aside>

### END PHASE

The game enters the end phase when a player has **gin**, or **knocks**.

#### Gin

When a player gets gin, at the end of their turn, they signal end of game by putting their discard card **face** **down** on the **upcard** pile and declaring, “Gin!”

Players reveal their hands. The player who got gin wins **20 points + the opponent’s total deadwood points**. The round ends.

<image example with caption/annotation of total points>

#### Knock

When a player has a deadwood value totaling 10 or less in their hand (0 deadwood would be gin), they have the option to “knock.” You knock when you fear that the other player may get gin before you.

1. **Knock**: At the end of their turn, a player signals their knock by putting their discard card **face** **down** on the **upcard** pile. 
    
    They declare: **“I knock with X,”** where X is their total deadwood. 
    
    To knock, your deadwood must total 10 points or less.
    
2. **Reveal**: Each player reveals their hand, organized by melds and deadwood.
    
    <image>
    
3. **Lay off deadwood:** The knocker’s ***opponent*** now has the opportunity to “lay off” (get rid of) their deadwood on the knocker’s melds: 
    - They regard the **knocker’s** **completed melds,** and if any of their deadwood can ***join*** those existing melds, they can do so.
    
    The round ends.
    
    Note: The player cannot break up or reconfigure the melds in any way; deadwood can only join *existing* straights or matches.
    

#### Calculating knock outcomes

There are two possible outcomes after laying off deadwood:

- **Deadwood Diff**. If the knocker’s deadwood total remains lower than the other player’s deadwood cost, the knocker wins the round with points totaling the *difference* between total deadwood costs.
    
    Example: If I knock with 9 total deadwood, and after the laying off phase my opponent has 23 total deadwood, I win 23 - 9 = 14pts, this round.
    
- **Cut**. It is possible for the knocker’s opponent to result in deadwood whose total is less than the knocker’s total deadwood. (It is possible to have started this way before laying off and after laying off, and even possible for the player to lay off *all* their deadwood this way.)
    
    If this happens, that player would “**cut,**” winning the round with **10 points + the difference between total deadwood**.
    
    Example: If I knock with 5 total deadwood, and after the laying off phase my opponent has 3 total deadwood, my opponent wins 10 points + (5 - 3) = 12pts, this round.

# Strategy and drops of wisdom

Drawing: Any card you pick should be to create a meld. If no meld is possible, it’s strategic to pick a lower-cost card to reduce total deadwood. You don’t want to be stuck with high cost deadwood if your opponent knocks or gets gin.
