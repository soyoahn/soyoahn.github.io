I continued to write features and art reviews for the newspaper throughout my freshman and sophomore years. --- # IBM Software Documentation Although _preventing_ user frustration (through good software design, UX, development, and testing) is the ideal place to begin, my primary expertise is in developing software documentation to develop useful mental schemas, bridge knowledge gaps, provide tips, and design the best steps for users. I write for a variety of audiences, including application developers, system administrators, and end users. My deliverables include: user interface (UI) strings, error messages, tutorials, scenarios, technotes, and other software documentation. I enjoy writing useful material that is easy to use and understand. I author information with Darwin Information Typing Architecture (DITA) in Arbortext Editor and Oxygen Editor.
