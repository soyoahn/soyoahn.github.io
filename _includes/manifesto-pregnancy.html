My Pregnancy Manifesto

Pregancy, birthing, and parenthood is SO WILDLY DIFFERENT for each person. A guide is almost meaningless. I'm going to try to word things so you're constantly reminded that my page represents a sample size of one. I am not going to pretend that this experience will be similar at all for you. But I do write it in hopes that it might help one person feel a little less anxious about the process. I wrote this in hopes to share it with anyone who reaches out for advice and recommendations. It'll all be right here and you can reach out to learn more--I'm an open book on this subject. I like to keep certain things private, but I believe this type of info is meant to be shared among women who seek to learn more about real experiences. This is not medical advice. It's a personal account.

Assumptions: If you have a vacation "away" message, then you will find some of my manifesto relatable and useful. If your first thought is "wtf is an OOTO message," this post will probably look cuckoo bananas, and I do apologize that our circumstances are so different. The inequity that so many experience in child rearing makes my heart sick.

Critical note about free will: Some (coworkers? potential employers?) will look at this post and deem it totally inappropriate to share within the same space as my resume. If you think that might be you, I want to thank you again for looking at my site, but please consider whether this content will offend you. If you think it will, please exercise your own free will and return Home. I promise you won't miss anything.

About my pregnancy and birthing experience: Slight anemia in early pregnancy, induced at 41wks, vaginal birth, no epidural.
