I write for a variety of audiences, including application developers, system administrators, and non-technical end users. My deliverables include: user interface (UI) strings, error messages, tutorials, scenarios, technotes, and other software documentation. I enjoy writing useful material that is easy to use and understand. I author information with Darwin Information Typing Architecture (DITA) in Arbortext Editor and Oxygen Editor. External users needed an easy-to-use, complete guide that could quickly get them started with the product. Previously, the information for getting started was scattered among several different places in the information center and users had trouble finding the right information to get up and running quickly. This guide needed to be especially easy to use, understand, and complete quickly. Customer calls on this issue grew costly and it was determined that we needed a more efficient, thorough guide that contained only the most basic, essential user tasks. I partnered with my technical editor and developer to turn the various pieces into one clear, self-contained "getting started" guide. We ended up completing the guide with praise and flying colors. Soon after the project was complete, the developer I had been working with sent it to a team member who was already seeking the type of information covered by the guide. After he sent the documentation, the colleague responded with satisfaction for the "well-written" guide that was both clear and useful.
